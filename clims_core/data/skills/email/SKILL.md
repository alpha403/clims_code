## PHASE 1 — RESEARCH (run all 3 tracks in parallel)

### Track A — Audience & Offer Research
- Who is this audience and what is their exact awareness level when they join this list?
- What do they want most? What do they fear most? What language do they use?
- What is the core offer — what transformation does it promise and in what timeframe?
- What are the top 3 objections that would stop them from taking action?
- What does "success" look like for this audience — what specific outcome do they want?

### Track B — Email Copywriting Benchmarks
- Fetch and read top email copywriting resources for this vertical (blogs, swipe files, case studies)
- What subject line formulas get the highest open rates in this niche?
- What email length, format, and CTA placement converts best for this offer type?
- What is the optimal send cadence for this sequence type (welcome: daily; nurture: every 2-3 days; re-engagement: every 5 days)?
- Fetch 2-3 example sequences from top brands in this space — note structure, tone, and hook patterns

### Track C — Deliverability & Compliance
- What subject line patterns trigger spam filters in major ESPs (avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, trigger words)?
- What preheader text length is optimal (40-90 characters)?
- Required compliance elements: unsubscribe link, physical address (CAN-SPAM/GDPR)?
- Plain text vs HTML — what works better for this audience?

- **Email 5:** [purpose — e.g. hard CTA + urgency/scarcity]

Adjust arc based on sequence type and number of emails requested.

---

## PHASE 3 — WRITE EVERY EMAIL

For each email write all four components:

**Subject line:** (write 3 options — curiosity / benefit / social proof)
**Preheader:** (40-90 chars, complements the subject line, never repeats it)
**Body:** (real copy, no placeholders — open with a hook, deliver the value/story/proof, transition to the CTA)
**CTA:** (one per email, specific, action-verb first — "Book your free call", "Download the guide", "Claim your spot")

### Email format rules:
- Short paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max, generous line breaks
- No walls of text — if a paragraph hits 4 lines, break it
- Plain conversational tone — write like a human, not a press release
- Every email has ONE job and ONE CTA — no multiple asks
- P.S. line on sales emails (readers scan to the bottom — put a second hook here)
- Open rate is won in the subject line; conversion is won in the first sentence of the body

## PHASE 4 — SUBJECT LINE VARIANTS

For the primary sales email (or the highest-stakes email in the sequence), write 5 additional subject line variants for A/B testing:
1. Curiosity / open loop
2. Specific benefit + timeframe
3. Social proof / numbers
4. Fear / consequence
5. Personal / direct ("I need to tell you something")

## SELF-CHECK (before delivering)

- [ ] Every subject line is under 50 characters (mobile preview)
- [ ] Every email opens with a hook — not "Hi [First Name], I hope you're well"
- [ ] Each email has exactly ONE CTA
- [ ] No email longer than 300 words unless it's a story/case study email
- [ ] Objections addressed before the CTA, not after
- [ ] Tone is consistent throughout the sequence
- [ ] Every email could stand alone if read out of sequence
- [ ] P.S. line included on all sales emails
- [ ] No placeholder copy — every word is real and ready to send
