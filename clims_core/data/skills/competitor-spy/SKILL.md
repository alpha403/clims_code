Research and produce a complete competitor intelligence report for the following:

**Brief:** $ARGUMENTS

If the brief is missing details, ask for:
- **Brand / niche:** what market are we auditing?
- **Competitors to analyse:** specific names, or should research identify them?
- **Focus areas:** ads / content / SEO / pricing / positioning / website / all
- **Goal:** find gaps to exploit, benchmark our performance, prepare to launch, understand the market

---

## PHASE 1 — COMPETITOR IDENTIFICATION

If competitors are not named in the brief, research them first:
- Google "[niche] [service/product]" and note the top 5-10 organic results
- Search Meta Ads Library for "[niche keywords]" and identify active advertisers
- Search YouTube and TikTok for top creators in this space
- Search "[niche] Reddit" and "[niche] Facebook group" for brands members recommend

Narrow to the top 5 most relevant competitors. For each, note: name, URL, primary offer, apparent target audience.

---

## PHASE 2 — DEEP RESEARCH (run one agent per competitor in parallel)

For each of the 5 competitors, run a dedicated research agent covering all of the following:

### Website & Positioning Audit
- Fetch their homepage. Note: headline (value proposition), subheadline, hero CTA, social proof in hero, offer structure
- What is their primary differentiator — what do they claim to do better than anyone else?
- What is their pricing model? Visible on site or hidden?
- How long is their homepage? What sections appear in what order?
- What trust signals are present: reviews, logos, certifications, guarantees, numbers?
- What is their primary conversion goal: book a call / buy / sign up / download?
- Website speed and mobile experience: does it load fast? Is mobile UX smooth?

### Ad Intelligence
- Search Meta Ads Library for their brand name and keywords — fetch all active ads
- How many ads are they running? (Many ads = actively testing; 1-2 ads = not scaling or paused)
- Which ads have been running longest? (Longevity = profitable — study these closely)
- What creative formats: UGC, static, video, carousel?
- What hooks are they leading with?
- What offers appear most in ads: free call, free guide, direct sale, trial?
- What landing pages do their ads point to? Fetch and analyse each

### Content Strategy Audit
- Fetch their Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube profiles
- Posting frequency on each platform
- What content pillars do they post in? What's missing?
- What are their 3-5 highest-engagement posts? What made them pop?
- What is their comment sentiment? (Read comments — what do followers love/complain about?)
- Do they use UGC, testimonials, behind-the-scenes? How?

### SEO & Keyword Audit
- Fetch their blog/resources section — what topics do they publish on?
- What keywords appear in their title tags and meta descriptions?
- Do they rank for the primary keywords in this niche? (search those terms and check their position)
- What backlink sources are they getting? (search "[brand name] site mentions")

### Offer & Funnel Audit
- What is their core offer? Price point if visible?
- Do they have a lead magnet? Fetch it or note what it promises
- What does their email sequence look like? (opt in and observe for 3-5 days — note or describe the first email)
- What upsells, cross-sells, or packages do they offer?

## PHASE 3 — SYNTHESIS (run after all competitor agents complete)

### Competitive Matrix
Build a comparison table across all 5 competitors:

| Brand | Core offer | Price | Primary audience | Main ad angle | Top content format | Lead magnet | Key strength | Key weakness |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Competitor A | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

### Market Positioning Map
Identify where each competitor sits on two key axes (choose axes relevant to the niche, e.g. price vs speed, generalist vs specialist, local vs global). Note the white space where our brand can own a unique position.

### Gap Analysis
**Content gaps:** topics no competitor is covering well (opportunity to dominate)
**Ad angle gaps:** emotional entry points nobody is using in ads
**Offer gaps:** price points, formats, or audiences completely unserved
**SEO gaps:** keywords with search volume that competitors don't rank for
**Audience gaps:** sub-segments the market is ignoring

---

## PHASE 4 — ACTIONABLE INTELLIGENCE REPORT

Deliver the final report in this structure:

### Executive Summary (5 bullets max)
The 5 most important findings and what to do about each — immediately actionable.

### What The Market Is Doing
Patterns across all competitors: what everyone is saying, selling, and showing. This is what we must differentiate from.

### The Opportunity
The specific gap in the market — the angle, audience, offer, or format that nobody is owning — and why it's an opportunity right now.

### Swipe File
The 5-10 best ads, posts, hooks, or headlines found during research. Not to copy — to learn from and outperform.

### Recommended Next Actions
Prioritised list of actions based on findings:
1. [Highest-leverage action] — e.g. "Launch an ad using the [identified gap] angle — no competitor is doing this"
2. [Second action]
3. ...
